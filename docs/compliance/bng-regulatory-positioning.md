# Biodiversity Net Gain: regulatory positioning and defensible claims

Astragrid Technologies Ltd, BioCoda. Version 1.0, July 2026.

This note keeps our product and sales claims accurate against the regulatory
framework, so we describe BioCoda truthfully and do not imply statutory authority
we do not have.

## The framework in brief

- **Environment Act 2021** introduced mandatory Biodiversity Net Gain in England.
  Since 2024, most new development must deliver at least a **10 percent** measurable
  gain in biodiversity, secured and maintained for **at least 30 years**.
- Gain is measured with the **statutory biodiversity metric** published by the
  Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs (Defra) and Natural England.
- Off-site gains are recorded on the **biodiversity gain sites register**
  administered by Natural England.
- The obligation is secured through planning conditions, planning obligations
  (Section 106), or conservation covenants, and monitored over the 30-year period.

## What BioCoda is

- A **monitoring and evidence** platform. It tracks habitat condition over time
  using Earth observation and authoritative field verification, flags parcels that
  are falling behind their required trajectory, and produces timestamped evidence.
- A tool that **references** the Defra biodiversity metric bands (poor, moderate,
  good) so its condition signal maps cleanly to the metric.

## What BioCoda is not (say this plainly)

- It is **not** the statutory biodiversity gain sites register and does not
  register sites with Natural England.
- It does **not** calculate or recalculate the statutory biodiversity metric. It
  links to metric outputs; the metric assessment remains the responsibility of a
  competent person.
- It does **not** create or discharge the legal agreements (planning conditions,
  Section 106, conservation covenants) that secure a gain.
- Its Earth observation output is **decision support, not determination**. Field
  verification by a competent ecologist is authoritative.
- It does **not** replace the statutory duties of a Local Planning Authority,
  responsible body, or developer, or the judgement of a professional ecologist.

## Defensible claims (safe to use)

- "Monitors Biodiversity Net Gain habitat condition from space and turns it into
  regulator-ready evidence."
- "Scores each parcel against its required 30-year recovery trajectory and flags
  what is falling behind."
- "Earth observation for early warning; field verification for authoritative
  confirmation."

## Claims to avoid

- Anything implying BioCoda is an official, statutory, or government system.
- Anything implying it calculates the statutory metric or guarantees compliance.
- Precision claims beyond what the imagery supports (Sentinel-2 is 10 metre
  resolution; small parcels and fine features have real limits).

## Accuracy and limitations to disclose

- Sentinel-2 optical imagery is 10 metre native resolution. Small parcels and fine
  features (hedgerows, individual trees) are inherently limited, which is exactly
  why field verification is positioned as authoritative.
- Cloud cover and season affect optical Earth observation; the model uses
  growing-season windows.
