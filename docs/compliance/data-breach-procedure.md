# Personal data breach response procedure

Astragrid Technologies Ltd, BioCoda. Version 1.0, July 2026. Owner: misi@biocoda.uk.

A personal data breach is any breach of security leading to accidental or unlawful
destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorised disclosure of, or access to, personal
data. This procedure ensures we contain it, assess it, and meet the UK GDPR
72-hour reporting duty.

## Roles

- **Incident lead:** the Astragrid data protection contact (misi@biocoda.uk).
- Everyone who suspects a breach must report it to the incident lead immediately.

## Steps

### 1. Report and record (immediately)

Anyone aware of a suspected breach notifies the incident lead without delay. The
lead opens a breach record with the time, who reported it, and what is known.

### 2. Contain (within hours)

Take immediate action to limit the breach: revoke compromised credentials, rotate
keys, disable affected access, isolate the affected component. Preserve evidence
and logs.

### 3. Assess the risk

Establish what data, whose data, how much, and the likely consequences for the
people affected. Record the assessment.

### 4. Notify the Information Commissioner's Office (within 72 hours)

If the breach is **likely to result in a risk to people's rights and freedoms**,
report it to the ICO within 72 hours of becoming aware, even if not all facts are
known. If it is unlikely to result in a risk, record the decision and the reason
not to report.

### 5. Notify affected individuals

If the breach is **likely to result in a high risk** to individuals, tell them
without undue delay, in plain language, with what happened and what they can do.

### 6. Notify affected customers

Where the breach concerns data we process on a customer's behalf, notify that
customer without undue delay, as required by the customer Data Processing
Agreement, so they can meet their own obligations as controller.

### 7. Review

After resolution, record the root cause, the actions taken, and lessons learned.
Update controls and this procedure as needed.

## Breach log

Every breach and suspected breach is logged, whether or not it is reported, with:
date/time aware, description, data and people affected, risk assessment, decision
on notification, actions taken, and outcome. The log is retained as evidence of
compliance.

## Provider incidents

Our sub-processors (Supabase, Vercel, Brevo, Cloudflare, Web3Forms) are
contractually required to notify us of breaches affecting our data. On such a
notice we run this procedure from step 3.
