# Data Processing Agreement (customer-facing)

This Data Processing Agreement (DPA) forms part of the agreement between the
customer (the **Controller**) and Astragrid Technologies Ltd (the **Processor**)
for the provision of BioCoda. It reflects UK GDPR Article 28.

Version 1.0, July 2026. This is a template to offer customers; have it reviewed by
your legal adviser before signing.

## 1. Subject matter and duration

The Processor provides BioCoda, a Biodiversity Net Gain monitoring service. This
DPA applies for as long as the Processor processes personal data on the
Controller's behalf under the agreement.

## 2. Nature and purpose of processing

Hosting, storing, and making available the Controller's account and habitat
monitoring data so authorised users can monitor habitat condition, record field
verifications, and produce evidence.

## 3. Types of personal data and data subjects

- **Data subjects:** the Controller's authorised users (staff, ecologists, agents).
- **Personal data:** name, email, organisation, role, authentication data, and
  field verification records (assessment, notes, and optional device location).
- No special category data is processed.

## 4. Controller instructions

The Processor processes personal data only on the Controller's documented
instructions, including the configuration of the service, unless required by law
(in which case it will inform the Controller unless legally prohibited).

## 5. Confidentiality

The Processor ensures that people authorised to process the personal data are
under an appropriate duty of confidentiality.

## 6. Security

The Processor implements appropriate technical and organisational measures,
including tenant isolation enforced by Row-Level Security, least-privilege access,
invite-only provisioning, encryption in transit and at rest, and access logging.
Details are in the security overview.

## 7. Sub-processors

The Controller gives general authorisation for the Processor to use the
sub-processors listed in the sub-processor register. The Processor will inform the
Controller of any intended change and give the Controller the opportunity to
object. The Processor remains liable for its sub-processors' compliance.

## 8. Assistance

The Processor assists the Controller, taking into account the nature of
processing, in: responding to data subject rights requests; keeping data secure;
notifying breaches; carrying out DPIAs; and consulting the ICO where required.

## 9. Data subject requests

If the Processor receives a request directly from a data subject, it will not
respond itself (save to acknowledge and redirect) but will forward it to the
Controller promptly.

## 10. Personal data breach

The Processor notifies the Controller without undue delay after becoming aware of
a personal data breach affecting the Controller's data, with the information the
Controller needs to meet its own obligations.

## 11. Return or deletion

On termination, at the Controller's choice, the Processor returns or deletes the
personal data it processes on the Controller's behalf, and deletes existing
copies, unless legally required to retain them.

## 12. Audits

The Processor makes available information necessary to demonstrate compliance with
Article 28 and allows for and contributes to audits, including inspections, by the
Controller or an auditor it mandates, on reasonable notice and subject to
confidentiality.

## 13. International transfers

Where personal data is transferred outside the United Kingdom, the Processor
ensures an appropriate safeguard is in place (a UK adequacy decision or standard
contractual clauses / the International Data Transfer Agreement).

## 14. Liability and governing law

Liability is as set out in the main agreement. This DPA is governed by the law of
England and Wales.
