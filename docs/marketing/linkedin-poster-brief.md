# BioCoda product poster: Claude Design brief

A single product poster for the **Astragrid LinkedIn page** introducing BioCoda.
One image, one message, built to stop the scroll and send people to
**www.biocoda.uk** (the canonical BioCoda website for all marketing, now and in
future briefs).

House style, non-negotiable: no em-dashes anywhere, and spell out acronyms in
full (Biodiversity Net Gain, not BNG; Earth observation, not EO).

---

## 1. What this poster is

One static image for the LinkedIn feed, posted from the Astragrid company page.
It introduces BioCoda to people who have never heard of it: a monitoring platform
that verifies England's 30-year Biodiversity Net Gain commitments from space and
turns satellite passes into regulator-ready evidence.

It is a poster, not a carousel and not an ad with a button, so the image itself
must carry the brand, the message, and the website address.

## 2. Format

- Primary: **1200 x 1200 px** square (best feed presence on mobile).
- Also export **1200 x 627 px** landscape for reuse in link posts.
- Everything legible at phone-thumbnail size; if a line does not read at a
  glance, cut words until it does.

## 3. The layout (this is the specific request, follow it exactly)

- **Background:** the supplied golden-hour photograph (rolling English valley at
  sunset, stream, wildflower hillside, bumblebees on a pink bergamot flower in
  the right foreground). Use the image full-bleed.
- **The fade:** apply a horizontal gradient scrim over the image, **strongest on
  the left and fading out to the right**. Left edge roughly 85 to 90 percent
  opaque, dissolving to fully transparent by about 55 to 60 percent of the
  width, so the bees and flower on the right stay vivid and untouched. Scrim
  colour: deep ink green-black (around #101913) rather than pure black, so the
  fade feels like dusk rather than a grey overlay.
- **Logo:** the BioCoda lockup (trajectory mark + wordmark, "Bio" forest green,
  "Coda" orchid purple) sits **top left**, inside the faded zone, fully clear of
  any busy imagery. Use the light-on-dark version of the wordmark if contrast
  needs it; never squash or recolour the mark.
- **Text block:** sits **beneath the logo, left-aligned**, inside the faded
  zone. Nothing but the fade behind it; no boxes, no pills.
- **Website:** **www.biocoda.uk** anchored bottom left, small but crisp, in
  white or pale lichen (#EFF3EC).
- Keep the right third of the poster text-free so the photograph breathes.

## 4. The text (punchy, pick one headline)

Headline options, in order of preference:

1. **"Nature made a promise. We watch it keep it."**
2. **"30 years of habitat recovery. Watched from orbit."**
3. **"Proof, not promises."**

Support line (one only, smaller, beneath the headline):

- "BioCoda monitors every Biodiversity Net Gain parcel from space and turns
  recovery into regulator-ready evidence."

Optional micro-line above the headline (eyebrow, all caps, letterspaced, orchid
or pale green): "BIODIVERSITY NET GAIN, VERIFIED".

Hierarchy: eyebrow (optional) then headline, big and bold, then support line,
then the website at the bottom. Four text elements maximum on the whole poster.

## 5. Brand kit

- Forest green #3B7D3C (primary, on-track, brand)
- Leaf green #2F6B30 (depth, hover)
- Orchid purple #8E5BB5 (the "Coda" accent; attention)
- Canvas / pale lichen #EFF3EC (light text on the dark fade)
- Type: Inter or close match; strong weight contrast, calm and confident.
- Green means healthy, orchid means attention; never swap those meanings.

## 6. Tone and what to avoid

Confident, credible, quietly clever. Earth-observation intelligence, not
eco-charity. Avoid: cartoon leaves, recycling-arrow motifs, rainbow gradients,
stock hands holding seedlings, and any text sitting on the busy right side of
the image.

## 7. Checklist before delivery

- [ ] Fade runs left (dark) to right (clear); bees and flower unobstructed.
- [ ] Logo top left, correct colours, fully legible on the fade.
- [ ] One headline, one support line, website bottom left: www.biocoda.uk.
- [ ] No em-dashes; acronyms spelled out in full.
- [ ] Legible at thumbnail size; both 1200x1200 and 1200x627 exported.
