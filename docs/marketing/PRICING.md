# BioCoda pricing (canonical)

This is the single source of truth for how BioCoda is priced. The landing-page
brief and the design brief both reference this document; if they disagree with
it, this wins.

House style: no em-dashes; acronyms spelled out (Earth observation, not EO;
Biodiversity Net Gain, not BNG).

**All figures below are indicative starting points for validation, not researched
market rates and not financial advice.** See "Assumptions" at the end. Numbers are
in pounds sterling, per year, excluding Value Added Tax, unless stated otherwise.

---

## 1. The model, in one paragraph

BioCoda is sold as an **annual per-organisation licence, scaled by the size of the
portfolio under monitoring**. The scaling meter is **hectares under monitoring**
(the parcel is the operational unit you see in the app; the hectare is what the
price scales on, because satellite monitoring cost tracks area). **Earth
observation monitoring is the core of every licence, not an add-on.** Field
verification is a separate module for organisations that want BioCoda to
coordinate and record ground-truth surveys. Every engagement starts with a
**paid pilot** on the customer's own sites and plans, credited against the first
year if they convert. It is sales-led, not a self-serve checkout.

So the three earlier descriptions reconcile like this:

| Earlier wording | Reconciled meaning |
| --- | --- |
| "Annual licence per organisation, scaled by portfolio size" | The licence and billing frequency: one annual licence per organisation. |
| "Per-parcel / per-hectare annual monitoring" | How that licence scales: by hectares under monitoring (parcels shown in-app). |
| "Earth observation monitoring module" | The core, included in every tier. |
| "Field-verification module" (add-on) | A genuine add-on, priced on top. |
| "Grant-funded pilot pathway" | The paid pilot, with a grant-funded route for eligible bodies. |

## 2. Tiers

Four tiers: one entry pilot and three annual licences. Caps are on hectares under
monitoring; the parcel count is a guide, not a hard limit.

### Tier 0: Pilot (entry point)
- **£4,000 one-off**, for a single scheme or site, up to **50 hectares** and about
  **20 parcels**, run for 3 months.
- Includes the full platform, Earth observation monitoring, one plan ingestion,
  and one branded evidence pack.
- **100 percent credited** against a Year-1 annual licence if you convert within
  60 days of the pilot ending.
- Grant-funded route available for eligible responsible bodies and Local Planning
  Authorities.

### Tier 1: Steward
For a small responsible body or a single scheme.
- **£6,000 / year**
- Up to **100 hectares** / about **40 parcels**
- Core Earth observation monitoring, annual evidence packs, plan ingestion,
  **3 user seats**
- Overage: **£40 per hectare / year** above the cap
- Effective rate at cap: about £60 per hectare / year

### Tier 2: Portfolio
For developers, habitat-bank operators, and mid-size responsible bodies.
- **£18,000 / year**
- Up to **500 hectares** / about **200 parcels**
- Everything in Steward, plus **quarterly monitoring cadence**, **10 seats**, data
  export and Application Programming Interface access, and priority email support
- Overage: **£30 per hectare / year** above the cap
- Effective rate at cap: about £36 per hectare / year

### Tier 3: Authority (enterprise / Local Planning Authority)
For Local Planning Authorities and large land managers monitoring many schemes.
- **From £45,000 / year**, custom
- **500+ hectares**, multi-organisation and authority-wide oversight
- Unlimited seats, single sign-on, tenant isolation and United Kingdom data
  residency assurances, custom service levels, onboarding and training
- Volume per-hectare pricing that steps down toward **£15 to £20 per hectare /
  year** at scale

## 3. Add-on modules (any tier)

- **Field verification module:** BioCoda coordinates and records ground-truth
  surveys and binds them to the evidence trail. **£3,000 / year** for unlimited
  coordination, or **£150 per dispatched survey** pay-as-you-go.
- **Additional evidence packs** beyond those included: **£250 each.**
- **Historical baseline backfill** (back-analysis of archive satellite imagery to
  establish a pre-BioCoda baseline): **one-off, from £1,500 per scheme.**
- **Single sign-on and advanced security** (included in Authority): **£3,000 /
  year** on lower tiers.

## 4. What every tier includes

Earth observation condition monitoring against the 30-year required trajectory,
at-risk flagging, the portfolio map, plan ingestion with provenance, branded and
timestamped evidence packs, invite-only least-privilege access with per-user
roles, and linkage to the Defra Biodiversity Metric (BioCoda references the
Metric; it does not recalculate it).

## 5. How the effective rate scales (sense check)

| Tier | Annual price | Cap (ha) | Effective £/ha/yr |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Steward | £6,000 | 100 | ~£60 |
| Portfolio | £18,000 | 500 | ~£36 |
| Authority | £45,000+ | 500+ | ~£15 to £20 |

The per-hectare rate falls as the portfolio grows, which is the intended
land-and-expand shape: small sites pay more per hectare because the platform base
dominates; large portfolios get volume pricing.

## 6. Positioning against the customer's own numbers

Frame the price as a small fraction of the liability it protects, never in the
abstract. Off-site biodiversity units trade in the tens of thousands of pounds
each, and a single scheme can need many; the 30-year management and monitoring
obligation is itself a budgeted, five-figure-per-year line item for larger sites.
BioCoda's monitoring licence sits inside that existing monitoring budget and de-
risks the whole 30-year commitment. That is the pitch: cheaper than one failed
audit, and it turns an unmonitorable liability into something you can see.

## 7. Assumptions (change these and the numbers move)

These tiers assume:
- The buyer is a United Kingdom organisation carrying or overseeing a Biodiversity
  Net Gain obligation, with monitoring already a budgeted cost.
- Target initial deal size in the low tens of thousands per year, landing at
  Steward or Portfolio and expanding.
- Satellite monitoring cost scales with area, so hectares are the fair meter.
- We are undercutting the cost of manual site-visit monitoring, not competing on
  being the cheapest tool.
