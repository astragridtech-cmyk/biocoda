ABOUT THE PRODUCT

BioCoda is Biodiversity Net Gain habitat monitoring software for the organisations that carry the 30-year obligation. It monitors every habitat parcel from space using Copernicus Sentinel-2 imagery, compares each one against the condition it is contracted to reach, classifies parcels as on track, at risk or awaiting a baseline, and directs field ecologists to exactly where they are needed. Their on-the-ground verification is the authoritative record. BioCoda ingests management plans, keeps a full audit trail, and exports timestamped, tamper-evident evidence packs. Earth observation is decision support; the authoritative condition assessment stays with the ecologist and the responsible body.

4) HOW IT WORKS (anchor: how-it-works)

- Heading: From plan to proof, in one place.

- Five numbered steps, each with a short title, one line of copy and a small icon:

  1. Bring in the plan or boundary. Extract every parcel, its baseline and target condition, the target year and the management actions from a management plan, with the source clause recorded. Or upload an Area of Interest boundary file.

  2. Set the required trajectory. Each parcel gets the minimum condition it must hold each year, rising from baseline to target and then held.

  3. Monitor from space. Copernicus Sentinel-2 imagery estimates condition continuously; every parcel is classified on track, at risk or awaiting a baseline.

  4. Verify on the ground. Dispatch a field survey to a flagged parcel; the ecologist's geotagged assessment becomes the authoritative record and can confirm or override the satellite signal.

  5. Prove it. Export a timestamped, tamper-evident evidence pack for any parcel, with before and after imagery, the condition metrics and the management progress.
