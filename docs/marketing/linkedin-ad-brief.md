# BioCoda LinkedIn ad campaign: design brief

For Claude Design. Goal: a small set of scroll-stopping LinkedIn ads (image +
copy) that introduce BioCoda to the people responsible for Biodiversity Net Gain
and get them to look at the product.

House style, non-negotiable: no em-dashes anywhere, and spell out acronyms in
full on anything a viewer reads (Biodiversity Net Gain, not BNG; Earth
observation, not EO).

---

## 1. What BioCoda is

BioCoda is a monitoring platform for Biodiversity Net Gain. In England, every
major development must deliver at least a 10 percent net gain in biodiversity and
maintain it for at least 30 years. Someone has to prove that habitats are
actually recovering, year after year, for three decades. Today that proof is
slow, manual, and easy to fudge: occasional site visits, spreadsheets, and taking
people at their word.

BioCoda replaces the guesswork with evidence. It tracks every habitat parcel from
space using Copernicus Sentinel-2 satellite imagery, scores its condition against
the required 30-year trajectory, flags parcels that are falling behind, and
produces a timestamped evidence pack you can hand to a regulator. In one line:

> **Biodiversity Net Gain you can actually see, verified from orbit, every year
> for 30 years.**

Live at **biocoda.astragrid.tech**.

## 2. Who we are talking to

Primary audiences on LinkedIn, in priority order:

1. **Responsible bodies and land managers** who hold the 30-year obligation and
   carry the risk if habitats fail.
2. **Local Planning Authorities** who must monitor and enforce gain across many
   sites with tiny teams.
3. **Developers and their ecology consultants** who need to evidence compliance
   without a site visit every quarter.

They are practical, slightly sceptical, time-poor, and allergic to greenwashing.
They respond to proof, not adjectives. Speak to the pain (a 30-year liability you
cannot see) and the relief (now you can).

## 3. Campaign objective

- Primary: awareness plus a click through to the site for a look or a demo.
- Tone: confident, credible, quietly clever. Environmental-tech, not eco-fluffy.
  Think Earth-observation intelligence, not a leaf-and-sunshine charity.
- One clear call to action per ad (see section 8).

## 4. Brand kit

Use these exactly. Consistency is the point.

**Logo / wordmark**: "BioCoda", with "Bio" in forest green and "Coda" in orchid
purple, alongside the trajectory mark (an upward-sweeping curved line, like a
recovery curve or a leaf stem). Master assets are in the repo; ask if you need
the SVG.

**Colours**
- Forest green `#3B7D3C` (primary, on-track, links, brand)
- Leaf green `#2F6B30` (darker green, depth and hover)
- Orchid purple `#8E5BB5` (the "Coda" accent, and the at-risk / attention colour)
- Canvas `#EFF3EC` (pale lichen background)
- Ink near-black for text, white for cards

Green means healthy and on-track. Orchid means attention or at-risk. Do not mix
that meaning up in a chart or a highlight.

**Type**: clean, modern sans-serif. Inter or a close match. Generous weight
contrast: big confident headlines, calm body text.

**Feel**: satellite imagery, real map data, precise UI, lots of breathing room. A
premium instrument, not a poster.

## 5. Deliverables

Design for LinkedIn's feed. Produce, at minimum:

1. **Three single-image ads** at **1200 x 627 px** (the 1.91:1 feed size). Three
   different angles from section 7 so we can test which lands.
2. **One square variant** at **1200 x 1200 px** of the strongest concept (square
   often wins more feed height on mobile).
3. Optional stretch: a **4 to 5 card carousel** at **1080 x 1080 px** that tells
   the story (the problem, the satellite view, the trajectory, the evidence pack,
   the call to action).

Keep any text well inside a safe margin. Assume it is viewed on a phone, so the
headline must be legible at thumbnail size.

## 6. Imagery direction

Lead with the product and real Earth. Options to draw from:

- **Split before / after satellite tiles** of the same parcel, showing bare
  ground greening into recovered habitat, with a subtle year stamp on each (for
  example "2026" and "2031"). This is the hero image.
- **The 30-year trajectory chart**: the actual required curve versus the observed
  condition line, green where on-track, orchid where at-risk. Very few marketing
  images show a 30-year line. That is the whole story in one shape.
- **The portfolio map** with parcels pinned on real satellite imagery, a couple
  glowing orchid to signal "this one needs attention".
- **The evidence pack**: a clean PDF page with the logo, before / after imagery,
  and hard numbers, shown at a slight angle like a document you can hand over.

Avoid: generic stock photos of hands holding soil, cartoon leaves, stock
butterflies, rainbow gradients, and anything that looks like a recycling logo.
Real satellite imagery and real UI beat any illustration here.

## 7. Copy directions (headlines plus support)

Each ad = one punchy headline on the image, plus a short LinkedIn post caption.
Pick the sharpest, do not crowd the image. Options:

**Concept A: the trust gap**
- Headline: "30 years is a long time to take someone's word for it."
- Caption: Biodiversity Net Gain is a three-decade promise. BioCoda checks it
  every year, from space, so on-track stays on-track and at-risk gets caught
  early. See it at biocoda.astragrid.tech.

**Concept B: verified from orbit**
- Headline: "Biodiversity Net Gain, verified from orbit."
- Caption: Every habitat parcel, scored against its 30-year trajectory with
  Copernicus Sentinel-2 imagery. No more taking recovery on trust. Book a look.

**Concept C: can you prove it**
- Headline: "You committed to 30 years of gain. Can you prove it?"
- Caption: Regulators want evidence, not intentions. BioCoda turns satellite
  passes into a timestamped evidence pack you can hand over with confidence.

**Concept D: see it recover**
- Headline: "Watch the hedgerow you plant this year recover, every year to 2056."
- Caption: BioCoda tracks each parcel from space and shows the recovery curve,
  green when on-track, so you manage by exception instead of by site visit.

Short taglines usable as a lockup near the logo: "Verified from orbit." /
"Biodiversity Net Gain you can see." / "Proof, not promises."

## 8. Call to action

One per ad. Preferred: **"See BioCoda"** or **"Book a demo"**, linking to
biocoda.astragrid.tech. Keep the button or end-card in forest green.
